Introduction to Welding

Welding is a fabrication process that joins materials, usually metals or thermoplastics, primarily by using high temperature to melt the parts together and allow them to cool, causing fusion. It involves melting the base metal and adding a filler material to form a weld pool.

Metal welding differs from lower temperature bonding techniques like brazing and soldering, which do not melt the base metal.

Welding requires precautions due to its hazardous nature, including protection against burns, electric shock, and exposure to ultraviolet radiation.

Overview of Major Welding Techniques

Arc Welding

Arc welding uses an electric arc to heat and join metals. It's versatile and widely used.

Oxy-Fuel Welding

Oxy-fuel welding uses acetylene and oxygen to create a hot flame for welding or cutting metals.

Shielded Metal Arc Welding

Also known as stick welding, it uses an electrode covered with flux to weld metals manually.
