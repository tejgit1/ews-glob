Engineering Workshop: Carpentry

Introduction to Carpentry

A carpenter is involved in shaping, cutting, and installing wood for buildings or smaller structures. They handle indoor and outdoor tasks using a variety of tools.

Carpenters prepare timber components for construction and install them in structures such as roofs, walls, floors, and other timber-framed structures.

They also construct household additions like skirting boards, doors, and architraves.

Skills and Tools Used

Skills

  • Interpreting blueprints and drawings
  • Designing and planning projects
  • Building and crafting wooden projects
  • Using tools accurately (rulers, levels, etc.)
  • Providing advice on timber selection
  • Maintaining and repairing wooden fixtures

Tools

Carpenters use a variety of hand tools and power tools:

  • Hand tools like claw hammers, chisels, and handsaws
  • Power tools including drills, circular saws, and routers
  • Organization tools like workbenches and tool belts
  • Accessories such as carpenter's pencils and miter boxes

Types of Carpentry

Rough Carpentry

Rough carpentry involves structural building of frameworks, such as posts, beams, and rafters.

Joister

Joisters specialize in constructing floor joists and related structures for flooring.

Trim Carpentry

Trim carpenters focus on building trims, moldings, and other detailed woodwork for rooms.

Cabinet Carpentry

Cabinet makers specialize in crafting cabinets and other woodworking furniture.

Ship Carpentry

Ship carpenters focus on building and maintaining ships, including modern vessels and replicas.

Framer

Framers specialize in constructing frameworks exclusively for housing and building projects.

Roofer

Roofer carpenters focus on roofing structures, including rafters, trusses, and beams.
