House Wiring: Electrical and Communication Systems

Introduction to House Wiring

In new home construction, wiring for electrical services and communication systems is installed before walls are finished. Existing buildings may require additional effort for concealed wiring, especially for new systems like security or home theater.

Multiple-unit dwellings like condominiums may have complex wiring needs to distribute services within each unit.

Services Commonly Found

  • Power points (wall outlets)
  • Light fixtures and switches
  • Telephone lines
  • Internet connections
  • Television (broadcast, cable, satellite)

High-End Features

  • Home theater systems
  • Distributed audio systems
  • Security monitoring (CCTV, burglar alarms)
  • Home automation and energy management systems

Power and Telecommunication Services

Electric power is typically supplied through overhead or underground cables into a distribution board (circuit breaker panel) mounted on a wall inside the home. The switchboard location is often outside the external wall of the garage in new homes.

Data services like internet are delivered using ADSL, cable modem, or fiber optic technologies, each requiring specific wiring configurations.

Information and Communication Systems

  • Entertainment systems wiring
  • Energy management systems
  • Security and safety systems
  • Digital home health monitoring
  • Intelligent lighting and power control

Specific Wiring Systems

Power Points

Installed throughout the house at appliance locations like kitchens, home theaters, and security systems.

Light Fittings and Switches

Varies by room and lighting requirements, may include multiway switches for corridors and stairwells.

Telephone Wiring

Required between the telephone company's service entrance and multiple outlets in the home.

Data Networking

Uses Cat 5 or Cat 6 cables for ADSL, cable modem, or fiber optic internet services, often configured in a star topology.

Television Wiring

Requires antennas, coaxial cables, and TV outlets for free-to-air or pay TV services. Can include satellite dishes or cable from the street.

Home Theater Pre-Wiring

Includes speaker cables and connections for audiovisual equipment, using cables like HDMI or DVI.

Distributed Audio Systems

Centralized music sources with speakers and volume controls throughout the house.

Security Systems and CCTV

Includes wiring for keypads, motion detectors, sirens, and CCTV cameras, often integrated with IP-based systems.
