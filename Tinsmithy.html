Engineering Workshop: Tinsmithy

Introduction to Tinsmithy

Tinsmithy involves the fabrication of various articles such as boxes, cans, funnels, and ducts from flat sheets of metal. The process includes drawing, cutting, and folding the metal to achieve the desired shapes.

Sheet metals like galvanized iron, black iron, tin, stainless steel, copper, and aluminum are commonly used in tinsmithy.

Hand Tools Used

The common hand tools used in tinsmithy include:

  • Steel try square
  • Wire gauge
  • Scriber for marking lines
  • Straight and curved snips for cutting sheet metal
  • Divider for transferring sizes and scribing curves
  • Bench vice for holding and bending workpieces
  • Lightweight hammers and mallets such as Ball Peen and Nylon Mallet

Types of Tools

Some specific tools used in tinsmithy:

  • Ball Peen Hammer: General purpose hammer for riveting in sheet metal work.
  • Cross Peen Hammer: Used for reaching awkward corners due to its tapered peen end.
  • Straight Peen Hammer: Convenient for folding operations with its parallel peen end.
  • Cutting Plier: Used for cutting, holding, and bending sheet metal.
  • Nylon Mallet: Lightweight mallet used for bending and folding work without damaging surfaces.
  • Stakes: Steel tools used to support, form, seam, bend, or rivet sheet metal objects.
  • Anvil: Provides support during hammering and bending operations.

Instructions and Safety Precautions

Instructions:

  • Draw the development of the object with true dimensions.
  • Use curved snips for trimming inside curves.
  • Always provide folding (hem) for top edges to avoid sharp edges and stiffen the sheet.
  • Properly ground exposed metal parts of electrically heated soldering irons.
  • Ensure metals to be joined are clean from dirt, grease, and oxide for good soldering.
  • Do not pull or peel cut portions by hand when using snips.
  • Use a nylon mallet with proper support for bending sheets.

Safety Precautions:

  • Use hand leather gloves while handling heavy sheets.
  • Avoid touching cut portions with bare hands when using snips.
  • Prevent sheets from slipping through hands.
  • Do not carry tools in pockets.
